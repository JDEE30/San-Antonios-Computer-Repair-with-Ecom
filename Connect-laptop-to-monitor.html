  • How to connect your laptop to a monitor

It's 2022, you just started a new job, and you can't figure out how to connect your laptop or desktop to your monitors. Well, you have a few options depending on your setup and your graphics card. If you need to combine several monitors, you might need an HDMI adaptor to connect the second monitor depending on your graphics card. If you need to connect one monitor, it's much more manageable. You need to find your HDMI cable and two HDMI ports. You will attach one end of the HDMI cable to the HDMI port in your laptop or desktop and the other end to your monitor. If for any reason you have trouble following this guide or it does not solve your issue just give us a call about our computer repair service, and we will be sure to fix your computer.

Where to find the hdmi port in your laptop

You can usually find your hdmi port on the back of your desktop, opposite of the power button , where your power cord is connected. Once you plug your hdmi cord into the hdmi port in the back of your desktop you can easily connect the other end into the hdmi port on the back or side of your monitor.

What is a monitor?

Your monitor looks like a tv; it has a power cord, buttons, and an HDMI port.

What is the laptop?

The desktop or laptop is also known as your computer or workstation. It is usually in the shape of a box or cube. Unless custom, it will look like a black box with a possible logo on the front from Dell, Asus, Acer, or some other brand. You will find the HDMI port on the back of this box.

My laptop does not have an hdmi connection

In some cases, you may need a dpi port, DVI port, VGA cord, or VGA port. The process to connect these are the same. You find the port that matches the shape of your cord and make sure your monitor is on the correct input; there is usually a menu button on the side of your monitor to choose the correct input. The input you choose should correspond with where you plugged in the cord. You can usually see that the port is labeled HDMI, VGA, dpi, hdmi2, etc...
